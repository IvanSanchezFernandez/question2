EEG1: From nonsensical wavy lines to differentiating wake from sleep

EEG may appear to the untrained eye as a bunch of noisy lines from which no information can be extracted. A sort of Rorschach test with ambiguous inkblots where what is seen depends largely on what is already in one's mind.



Fortunately, there is a lot of objective information in EEGs. It is all about pattern recognition. Many EEG newbies typically struggle to identify these patterns and do not even know where to start. This post will try to provide new EEG readers with some help on the most basic pattern recognition in EEG: identifying wakefulness and the different stages of sleep. Get ready.
